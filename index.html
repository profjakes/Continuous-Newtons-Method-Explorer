About the math

Continuous Newton's Method is the ODE z'(t) = −p(z)/p'(z). Trajectories satisfy p(z(t)) = p(z₀)·e⁻ᵗ, so they flow to a root while arg p(z) is preserved.

Cayley's classical map z ↦ z − p/p' is just the Euler step (h = 1). This app applies six different ODE schemes — Euler, Refined Euler, Heun, RK2 (optimal), RK4, and 2-step Adams–Bashforth — and colours each pixel by which root the orbit converges to. Brighter = faster convergence; black = no convergence within max iterations.

Reference: J. Jacobsen, O. Lewis, B. Tennis, "Approximations of Continuous Newton's Method: An Extension of Cayley's Problem," EJDE Conf. 15 (2007), pp. 163–173.
